    To me, progressive enhancement is making sure that all users are provided the essential content and functionality of a website. It also ensures that users with the most modern browsers, meaning the required code, get the best experience possible.I think progressive enhancement is important in modern web development because technology is constantly evolving and it's important to make websites accessible to many different types of devices. As the article stated, there's many layers to gathering your content and considering how functional it is. On older devices, it may not function as properly compared to newer devices. The same goes for older browsers as well. The most important thing to do is work with your content first then expand to css and js.

    According to this article, Progressive enhancement, what it is and how to use it, it's important to have a strong foundation to fall back on when as you continue to make your project more complex. And with today's society, almost everyone has a mobile device. So knowing how your website functions and looks on all type of devices is something that progressive enhancement is a part of. Some functions may not work on a phone or javascript may be disabled for some in places such as work or school. Progressive enhancement allows users to start with the complex and work on more layers as they go.
